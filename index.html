What is Autism?

Autism spectrum disorders (ASD) are a diverse group of conditions related to brain development. They are characterized by difficulties in social interaction and communication, as well as atypical patterns of behavior, such as repetitive activities or unusual reactions to sensations. The abilities and needs of individuals with autism can vary widely, and while some can live independently, others may require lifelong care and support. For better understanding, Autism is a Spectrum Disorder or disability. People with autism often lack understanding skills, meaning their mind develops differently. They have repetitive behaviors, experience anger issues, and can easily get triggered when facing discomfort. They often struggle with communication skills. They are individuals who need rigorous therapy and treatment to become ready for society or at least independent.

AutiCure

Keeping in mind the sensory sensitivities and the various spectrums, we have decided to develop a game named Autism Allies which addresses all the requirements of individuals according to their customization. Autism Allies is an innovative first-person game designed to train individuals with autism. This game provides an engaging gaming experience with therapy included. It's a highly customizable game that ensures user safety and comfort, designed to meet the unique needs and preferences of each player.

Key Features of Autism Allies

Full Customization

Customize every aspect of the game environment, characters, and audio to match individual preferences and sensory needs.

Therapeutic Mini-Games

Engaging activities designed to develop cognitive skills, communication, and daily living abilities.

Life Skills Simulation

Practice essential daily activities in a safe, virtual environment before applying them in real life.

Our Vision

Development Over Winning

The game is not about winning or losing - it's about the amount of development seen in the player's behavior. The game continues as long as the character makes correct choices.

Guided Learning

If choices are wrong in the simulation, the in-game guardian provides commands to guide the player toward the right path, ensuring continuous learning.

Positive Reinforcement

Correct choices are rewarded with preferred items like candies or chocolates. No matter how small the achievement, every positive choice is celebrated to encourage and motivate.

Our vision is to create a safe, encouraging environment where every small step forward is recognized and celebrated, building confidence and independence.
